When I first started thinking about freelancing, I knew I needed one thing more than anything else — a professional online presence. Not just a LinkedIn profile. A real website with my name on it.

The problem? I had zero budget.

So I decided to figure it out myself. And I did — completely free.

## What I Built

A full professional portfolio website with:

- Animated hero section with my photo
- Voice intro that speaks when visitors land on the page
- Skills, Experience, Projects, and Certifications sections
- A working contact form that sends messages directly to my Gmail
- A WhatsApp floating chat button
- A 3D "Hire Me" modal form
- A Thank You page after form submission

All of it — completely free. No hosting fee. No domain fee. No monthly bill.

## Tools I Used (All Free)

**GitHub Pages** — Free hosting forever. You push your HTML files to a GitHub repository and your site goes live at `username.github.io`. No credit card, no expiry.

**Formspree** — Free contact form backend. When someone fills your form, the message goes straight to your Gmail. Free plan allows 50 submissions per month which is more than enough when starting out.

**Font Awesome** — Free icons for the entire site.

**Google Fonts** — Free premium typography.

## The Biggest Lesson

You don't need money to start. You need consistency and the willingness to learn.

I went from zero to a live professional website in a few days of learning. Every problem I ran into, I researched, figured out, and solved. That problem-solving process itself is a skill that makes you a better marketer and developer.

If you're a freelancer with no budget — start here. Build your portfolio first. Everything else follows.
